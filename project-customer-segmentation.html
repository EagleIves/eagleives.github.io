Customer Segmentation and Purchase Behaviour Prediction

Using K-means clustering and association rules mining to segment customers and develop targeted marketing strategies

2024
Individual Project
Python, Scikit-learn

Project Overview

OBJECTIVE

My goal was to classify and segment customers based on their historical purchases and ultimately develop an accurate model for predicting their future purchasing behaviour. I wanted the data to tell me where this American shopowner should put their money to get the most out of their investment.

Methodology

1

Data Transformation

Categorical variables such as "Season" and "Payment Method" were converted to numerical format for clustering. Machine learning models work better with numerical inputs, so encoding these categories as numbers was essential. Using One-hot encoding, the "Payment Method" column was converted into binary columns (0/1) for model compatibility, helping detect patterns like "Cluster 0 prefers Credit Card payments."

2

Feature Selection for Clustering

Identified cluster features that collectively define customer behaviour across these categories:

  • Demographics: Age
  • Spending habits: Purchase Amount, Monetary, High_Spender
  • Engagement: Frequency, Recency, Loyal_Customer, Frequent_Buyer
  • Preferences: Discount_Lover, Promo_User
3

Clustering Analysis

Utilized the Elbow/silhouette methods to ensure selection of optimal clusters, avoiding oversegmentation from arbitrary choice of k. The optimal number of clusters identified was Four (4).

Elbow and Silhouette Methods for Optimal Cluster Selection

Grouped customers by behaviour (spending, loyalty, etc.) using K-means clustering - a simple, efficient algorithm for partitioning data into k clusters. Analysis of cluster profiles revealed what defines each cluster, computing mean values per cluster to generate actionable insights.

Normalized Characteristics of Each Customer Cluster

4

Association Rules Mining

To find frequently co-purchased items, employed the Apriori Algorithm with the following metrics:

  • Support: How frequently itemset appears (min 5%)
  • Lift: Measures dependence (lift > 1.2 means likely together)
  • Confidence: Probability of buying Y given X (min 50%)

Key Association Highlights:

  • • Customers who buy Coats often buy Gloves (lift 1.8)
  • • Handbag purchases frequently paired with jewellery (lift 1.7)
  • • Pants purchases often lead to Shirt purchases (lift 1.5)
